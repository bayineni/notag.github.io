Tagging Etiquette in Microsoft Teams

Purpose of Tagging

Tagging is a tool to:

  • Direct attention to specific individuals or groups.
  • Assign tasks or request actions.
  • Clarify context within conversations.
  • Enhance visibility of important messages.

Best Practices

  • Be intentional: Tag only when necessary.
  • Provide context: Explain why you're tagging someone.
  • Limit tags: Avoid tagging multiple people in a single message.
  • Respect privacy: Avoid tagging in private or 1:1 chats unless essential.
  • Use group tags: Prefer team or role-based tags over individual ones.

Common Pitfalls

  • Over-tagging: Tagging too many people can lead to notification fatigue.
  • Ambiguity: Tagging without context can cause confusion.
  • Interruptions: Tagging inappropriately can disrupt others' workflows.
